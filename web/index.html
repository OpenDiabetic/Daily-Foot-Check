How a check works

Three steps. About two minutes. Nothing left behind.

1

Capture

Four photos: the top and the bottom of each foot — ulcers form on the sole, so the bottom views matter most. Lighting and focus are checked on your phone before anything is analyzed.

2

Analyze

A medical-grade vision model looks at the photo and reports only what it can see — redness, dryness, swelling, nail changes — in plain words. It runs on your device or our machines: your choice, same result.

3

Learn

Observations are matched to our Field Guides, with a simple flag: green, yellow, or red. Red means one thing only — worth showing your care team. One tap builds a care package PDF you can send them yourself — tamper-proof, with a verification fingerprint printed on it. It is never a diagnosis.

Every check leaves a receipt. Never a photo.

Your report is reduced to a fingerprint (a hash). Each day, all fingerprints are combined and notarized twice — by a public network and by an independent timestamp authority. Anyone can check either one, forever. Neither contains your photo, your report, or your name.
